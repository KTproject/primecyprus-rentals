FAQ

Common questions about renting in Cyprus.

A modern 2-bedroom apartment in central or coastal Limassol typically ranges from €1,500 to €3,000 per month. Luxury villas in Germasogeia or Agios Tychonas range from €4,000 to €10,000+ per month. Short-term rentals command a premium over long-term lets.
Yes. There are no restrictions on foreigners renting in Cyprus. EU citizens can rent freely. Non-EU nationals can also rent without restrictions — a valid passport is typically required when signing a lease.
EU citizens can stay indefinitely. Non-EU nationals can stay up to 90 days within any 180-day period without a visa. For longer stays, a temporary residency permit is required. Many clients use Cyprus’s 60-day tax residency rule to structure their stays efficiently.
Check that the property has a valid title deed, whether utilities are included, air conditioning provision, parking, and proximity to amenities. For long-term rentals, ensure the lease is in writing and registered with the Cyprus Land Registry.
Yes. Cyprus ranks highly for expat quality of life. English is widely spoken, it is an EU member state with 320 days of sunshine per year, low crime rates, good healthcare, and a strong international community particularly in Limassol and Paphos. 3 hours by flight from most European cities.
Short-term rentals (days to a few months) are typically furnished and serviced, priced per night or week. Long-term rentals (6 months+) are usually unfurnished or semi-furnished, rented monthly, and significantly cheaper per month. Many relocating clients start short-term while searching for their long-term home.
