FAQ

Common questions about renting in Cyprus.

A modern 2-bedroom apartment in central or coastal Limassol typically ranges from €1,500 to €3,000 per month. Luxury villas in Germasogeia or Agios Tychonas range from €4,000 to €10,000+ per month.
Yes. There are no restrictions on foreigners renting in Cyprus. EU citizens can rent freely. Non-EU nationals can also rent without restrictions — a valid passport is typically required when signing a lease.
EU citizens can stay indefinitely. Non-EU nationals can stay up to 90 days within any 180-day period without a visa. Many clients use Cyprus’s 60-day tax residency rule to structure their stays efficiently.
Check that the property has a valid title deed, whether utilities are included, air conditioning provision, parking, and proximity to amenities. For long-term rentals, ensure the lease is in writing and registered with the Cyprus Land Registry.
Short-term rentals (days to months) are typically furnished and serviced. Long-term rentals (6 months+) are usually unfurnished or semi-furnished and significantly cheaper per month. Many relocating clients start short-term while searching for their long-term home.
